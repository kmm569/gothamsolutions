What this calculator does

This template helps you project future savings growth based on the inputs you already know. Instead of manually doing compound interest math, you can plug in your numbers and instantly see how different balances, contribution amounts, rates, and timelines affect your results.

It is built to be practical and easy to adjust, whether you are casually saving, building an emergency fund, comparing account options, or working toward a set goal amount.

Best for

  • Personal savings planning
  • Emergency fund tracking
  • High-yield savings account projections
  • CD and fixed-rate growth estimates
  • Goal-based saving timelines
  • Comparing contribution strategies

What you can enter

The calculator is designed around the inputs most people actually care about when planning savings growth.

Starting balance

Enter your current amount already saved so the projection starts from a realistic baseline instead of zero.

Recurring contributions

Add the amount you plan to contribute on a regular basis to simulate how consistent deposits impact long-term growth.

Interest rate

Input the annual rate or APY for the account so the sheet can calculate projected growth over time.

Compounding frequency

Test how growth changes with different compounding schedules such as monthly, daily, quarterly, or annually.

Time period

Set a savings timeframe to estimate the future value of your account based on your current plan.

Goal amount

Use a target balance to estimate how long it may take to reach your savings goal under your current settings.

Ways you can use it

  • See how much your balance could grow in 6 months, 1 year, 5 years, or longer
  • Compare saving more each month versus waiting longer
  • Estimate how long it could take to hit a target savings goal
  • Test how different account rates change your results
  • Compare general savings accounts, high-yield accounts, or CDs
  • Plan for large purchases, moving expenses, travel, or emergency reserves

Why use a spreadsheet version

A spreadsheet gives you more control than a basic online calculator. You can duplicate tabs, test multiple scenarios, keep your own notes, and make updates without starting over every time.

This is especially helpful if you want a tool you can revisit regularly instead of just getting a single one-time answer.

Good to know

This is an estimate

Results are projections based on the numbers you enter. Real account growth can vary depending on rate changes, deposit timing, account terms, and whether the institution changes its APY.
